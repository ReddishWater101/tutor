Homework 3 and Homework 4 are not missing everything, but they are not the whole exam either. In plain English, they cover a lot of the core mechanics, especially the main kinds of inventory math you have been practicing. Homework 3 gives you the newsvendor side, meaning how to think about stocking too much versus stocking too little. Homework 4 gives you the continuous replenishment side, meaning reorder points, order quantities, safety stock, and service levels. If you understand both homeworks well, you are covering a large and important part of the exam.

But there are still some gaps that could show up on the exam. The study guide is broader than the homework sets. It suggests you may need some basic probability comfort, demand-during-lead-time setup, z-table use, assumptions behind the models, and some explain-it or derivation-style understanding instead of only number crunching. Also, the exam announcement says the exam includes both full backlogging and lost sales for the (q,r) policy. Homework 4 mostly gives you full backlogging and service-level work, not a clean direct practice problem for the optimal lost-sales version of the (q,r) model. So that is one real gap.

`Exam2_MadeUpProblems.md` is actually pretty good and it lines up well with Homework 3 and Homework 4. It gives you single-period newsvendor thinking, multiple-period customer-waits versus customer-leaves thinking, a full-backlogging (q,r) problem, and service-level design and assessment. If you study that file really well and deeply, you will probably cover a big chunk of the exam material.

Still, I would not trust only that file by itself. The reason is that it is a little narrower than the full exam scope. It does not really give you the broader spread that `Exam2_Questions.md` gives you. In particular, `Exam2_Questions.md` gives you more variety in newsvendor style, including cost-minimization-flavored questions, and it gives you a direct lost-sales version of the (q,r) policy. That matters because the exam scope explicitly includes both backlogging and lost sales for (q,r), not just one of them.

So the honest answer is this: if you only study `Exam2_MadeUpProblems.md`, you might be okay if the exam leans heavily toward the same style of problems, but I would not call that safe. If you want the safer plan, study `Exam2_MadeUpProblems.md` deeply as your main file, then use `Exam2_Questions.md` to add breadth. The extra breadth you want is single-period cost-minimization thinking, the lost-sales version of the (q,r) model, and just more variety in how the same ideas can be asked.

If you are short on time, the most practical approach is this. First, know `Exam2_MadeUpProblems.md` cold. Second, make sure you can also do the lost-sales (q,r) problems and the cost-minimization style newsvendor questions from `Exam2_Questions.md`. Third, spend at least a little time reviewing the study-guide-style ideas that are not just calculations, especially demand during lead time, z-table use, Type I versus Type II service levels, and the basic meaning of the model assumptions. That plan gives you both depth and enough breadth to be much safer.
