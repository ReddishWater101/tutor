When to Use Which Critical Ratio Formula

Single period problems are when you order once and that is it. The item either sells or it does not, and leftovers are salvaged or thrown away. Think popcorn that gets tossed at the end of the night, holiday cards that are worthless after the holiday, or seasonal shirts that get clearanced. If the problem says the item cannot be stored for future use, or there is only one ordering opportunity, it is single period. Use c sub u equals p minus c plus b, and c sub o equals c minus v.

Multi period problems are when unsold items carry over to the next period and can still be sold later. Think of a bookstore that keeps unsold books on the shelf for next week, or a store that restocks a staple product on a regular cycle. The product does not expire or become worthless. Once you know it is multi period, you ask one more question: what happens when you run out? If customers are willing to wait and you fill their order later, that is full backlogging. Use c sub u equals b and c sub o equals h. If customers leave and buy from a competitor instead, that is lost sales. Use c sub u equals p minus c plus b and c sub o equals h. The giveaway is usually in the problem wording. Phrases like "willing to wait" or "backordered" mean full backlogging. Phrases like "find an alternative supplier" or "go elsewhere" mean lost sales.
