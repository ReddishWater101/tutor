# logistics
## 1. SunVolt Direct Shipments vs. Cross-Dock Network | Source: Modeled after Assignments/Homework_6_solution.pdf and Lectures/Lecture_8_Logistics-B.pdf
SunVolt assembles two battery backup products, P1 and P2, at facilities in Dallas and Phoenix. P1 requires 2 units of component C1 and 1 unit of component C2, and is assembled in Dallas at a rate of 70 units/day. P2 requires 1 unit of C1 and 3 units of C2, and is assembled in Phoenix at a rate of 90 units/day. C1 is manufactured in Detroit, and C2 is manufactured in Memphis. The assembly facilities operate 360 days/year.

SunVolt outsources transportation to a third-party logistics provider. For direct shipments from suppliers to assembly facilities, each truck can carry 4,500 units of either C1 or C2 and costs $2,800 per trip. The delivery lead time from Detroit to either assembly facility is 5 days, and the delivery lead time from Memphis to either assembly facility is 4 days. SunVolt incurs an on-site inventory holding cost of $0.18/unit/day and an in-transit inventory holding cost of $0.12/unit/day.

a. What are the total annual logistics costs if full truckload direct deliveries are made from the supplier plants to the assembly plants?

b. What are the total annual logistics costs if optimal shipment sizes are used for direct deliveries from the supplier plants to the assembly plants?

c. A consultant suggests using a cross-dock in Kansas City. Components would be shipped from Detroit and Memphis to Kansas City in full truckloads. Each inbound truck can carry 3,000 units of a single component type, costs $1,200 per trip, and has a 2-day lead time. From Kansas City, a separate truck would go to Dallas and Phoenix every 10 days carrying exactly 10 days of requirements for both components. Each outbound truck costs $1,600 per trip and has a 2-day lead time. Assume no inventory is held at the cross-dock. What are the total annual logistics costs under this cross-dock policy?

## 2. Bose and Target Channel Coordination | Source: Modeled after Lectures/Lecture_9_Supply Chain.pdf, Pages 31-35
Bose sells a particular headphone model through Target stores. Suppose that demand for this headphone model at one Target retail location is constant at 1,200 units/year. Both Bose and Target use a replenishment quantity approach similar to the supply chain coordination model discussed in class.

The unit cost of the headphone is $180. The fixed order cost for Bose is $4,500 per order, while the fixed order cost for Target is $90 per order. Bose uses an annual inventory holding rate of 20%, and Target uses an annual inventory holding rate of 35%.

a. If Bose has the channel power, calculate the replenishment order quantity. Calculate the ordering and holding costs incurred by Bose and Target.

b. If Target has the channel power, calculate the replenishment order quantity. Calculate the ordering and holding costs incurred by Bose and Target.

c. If Bose and Target integrate their decision making, determine the replenishment order quantity and calculate the total channel cost.

d. Which decision structure gives the lowest total channel cost?
