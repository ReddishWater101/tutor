SunTrail Manufacturing makes a replacement battery pack. Annual demand is
18,000 packs/year. Each production setup costs $1,200. The unit production
cost is $30, the annual inventory carrying rate is 25 percent, and the
production line can make 60,000 packs/year. The company operates 360 days
per year.

The purchasing department is also considering outsourcing the item to a
supplier that offers the following incremental discount schedule:

| Order quantity | Incremental unit price |
|---:|---:|
| 0 <= q < 5,000 | $34.00 |
| 5,000 <= q < 10,000 | $31.50 |
| 10,000 <= q | $29.75 |

If the item is outsourced, the fixed ordering cost is $900/order and the
supplier lead time is constant at 52 days.

a. If SunTrail produces the item internally, find the economic production
quantity, production cycle length in days, number of setups per year, and
average annual setup plus inventory holding cost.

b. Under the incremental discount scheme, find the candidate order
quantities for outsourcing.

c. Find the outsourcing order quantity with the minimum average annual total
cost, including procurement, ordering, and holding costs.

d. If SunTrail outsources using the order quantity from part (c), find the
reorder level.

# Problem 2 - Found in Exams/StudyGuide_Exam3.pdf, Exams/FormulaSheet_Exam3.pdf, Lectures/Lecture_4_Inventory-Newsvendor.pdf, Lectures/Lecture_5_Inventory-QR.pdf, Assignments/ProblemSet_2_Newsvendor.pdf, Assignments/ProblemSet_3_qrpolicy.pdf, Assignments/Homework_3_solution.pdf, Assignments/Homework_4_solution.pdf, Assignments/Homework_5_solution.pdf

Orange Lake Outfitters sells a seasonal rain jacket. Demand during the
season is normally distributed with mean 1,200 jackets and standard
deviation 240 jackets. Each jacket costs $42, sells for $75, and any leftover
jackets can be salvaged for $18 each. Management estimates a loss-of-goodwill
cost of $6 for each customer who wants a jacket but cannot buy one.

Orange Lake also sells a year-round umbrella. Weekly demand for the umbrella
is normally distributed with mean 90 units and standard deviation 25 units.
Lead time is random with mean 3 weeks and standard deviation 1 week. The
store operates 52 weeks/year. The umbrella costs $12/unit, the fixed ordering
cost is $160/order, and the annual inventory carrying rate is 30 percent. If
umbrella demand occurs when the store is out of stock, the sale is lost and a
shortage cost of $18/unit is charged.

a. For the seasonal jacket, identify the underage cost and overage cost, then
find the optimal single-period order quantity.

b. For the umbrella, find the mean and standard deviation of demand during
lead time.

c. Suppose Orange Lake wants a Type I service level of 96 percent for the
umbrella. Find the EOQ order quantity and the reorder level.

d. Suppose Orange Lake currently orders 900 umbrellas at a time and reorders
when the inventory position reaches 380 units. Find the implied Type I and
Type II service levels.

e. Using the iterative lost-sales (q, r) procedure for the umbrella, find the
optimal order quantity and reorder level. Stop when the z-value is stable to
two decimals.

# Problem 3 - Found in Exams/StudyGuide_Exam3.pdf, Exams/FormulaSheet_Exam3.pdf, Lectures/Lecture_7_Logistics-A.pdf, Lectures/Lecture_8_Logistics-B.pdf, Assignments/ProblemSet_4_Logistics.pdf, Assignments/Homework_6_solution.pdf

Marsh Tech buys component A from Tulsa and component B from Mobile. Both
components are used at two assembly plants: Phoenix and Tampa. Phoenix
assembles 45 units/day, and Tampa assembles 75 units/day. Each finished unit
requires 1 unit of component A and 2 units of component B. The plants operate
360 days/year.

Trucks carry up to 2,700 units of a single component. A direct supplier-plant
truckload costs $3,000 per trip and has a 5-day transit time. The on-site
inventory holding cost is $0.45/unit/day. The in-transit inventory holding
cost is $0.25/unit/day.

Marsh Tech is considering a warehouse in Dallas. Shipments from each supplier
to Dallas cost $3,600/truckload, have a 4-day transit time, and use the same
2,700-unit truck capacity. Shipments from Dallas to each plant may carry a
mixed load of components, cost $2,400/truckload, have a 2-day transit time,
and use the same 2,700-unit truck capacity. Inventory held at Dallas costs
$0.20/unit/day.

a. Calculate the annual logistics cost if Marsh Tech uses Dallas as a
warehouse. Assume each supplier sends full truckloads to Dallas, Dallas sends
full mixed truckloads to each plant, and average warehouse inventory equals
one-half of each inbound shipment quantity. Include transportation,
in-transit inventory, plant on-site inventory, and warehouse inventory costs.

b. Calculate the annual logistics cost if Dallas is operated as a pure
cross-docking facility. Assume inbound and outbound shipments are scheduled so
that no inventory is stored at Dallas, and all trucks are full.

c. Calculate the annual logistics cost if Marsh Tech keeps direct shipments
but uses optimal shipment sizes when they are less than truck capacity.
Include transportation, in-transit inventory, and plant on-site inventory
costs.

d. Which of the three policies is cheapest?

# Problem 4 - Found in Exams/StudyGuide_Exam3.pdf, Lectures/Lecture_9_Supply Chain.pdf Example 8.1

A manufacturer and a retailer sell a product with annual demand of 900
units/year. The unit cost is $500. For the manufacturer, the fixed order cost
is $6,400/order and the annual inventory carrying rate is 20 percent. For the
retailer, the fixed order cost is $400/order and the annual inventory
carrying rate is 32 percent.

a. If the manufacturer has channel power, find the replenishment order size.
Then calculate the manufacturer's cost, the retailer's cost, and the total
channel cost.

b. If the retailer has channel power, find the replenishment order size. Then
calculate the manufacturer's cost, the retailer's cost, and the total channel
cost.

c. If the manufacturer and retailer integrate their replenishment decision,
find the integrated order size. Then calculate the manufacturer's cost, the
retailer's cost, and the total channel cost at that integrated order size.

d. Compared with the manufacturer-driven and retailer-driven cases, how much
total channel cost does integration save?
