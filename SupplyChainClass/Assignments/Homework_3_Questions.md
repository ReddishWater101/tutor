2. [8 points] A movie theater sells a "flavor of the week" pop-corn on Saturday nights. They have been doing this for the past 6 months, and observed the demand for the "flavor of the week" popcorn to be normally distributed with a mean of 9,600 buckets and a standard deviation of 2,400 buckets. The cost of producing a bucket of flavored popcorn is $2.5 per bucket, and they are sold for $9.50 per bucket. The manager of the concession stand estimates that the unit loss of goodwill cost for their flavored popcorn is $3 per bucket. Any unsold flavored popcorn has to be discarded for composting.

a. [4 points] How many buckets of flavored popcorn should the concession manager plan for the next Saturday night?

b. [4 points] What should the unit loss of goodwill cost be, for the optimal number of buckets to be 12,000?


 

3. [8 points] A supplier sells white cotton fabric to manufacturers in its region on a monthly basis. Any white cotton fabric unused at the end of the month can be stock and sold during the next month. The quantity of fabric sold by the manufacturer was determined to be approximately normally distributed with a mean of 250 rolls and a standard deviation of 20 rolls. It costs the supplier $100 to produce a roll of white cotton fabric, and sells a roll for $120 per roll to its customers. The supplier estimates that the unit loss of goodwill per roll is $30. The supplier incurs an inventory holding cost of $5 per roll per month. 

a. [4 points] Suppose that that the clothing manufacturers who buy the white cotton fabric from the supplier are patient, and, hence, are willing to wait for a month if the supplier cannot immediately provide the rolls of cotton fabric they need.  What is the optimal order quantity for the supplier?

b. [4 points] Suppose that the clothing manufacturers who buy the white cotton fabric from the supplier am impatient, and, hence, find an alternative supplier immediately to obtain the rolls of cotton fabric they need. What is the optimal order quantity for the supplier?
