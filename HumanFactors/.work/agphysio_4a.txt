Two Types of Muscular Effort

 Ergonomists differentiate between two types of muscular effort:
1. Dynamic Effort:

     Involves rhythmic contraction and relaxation of muscles.
     Allows more blood to be circulated through the muscle, effectively meeting

      oxygen and waste removal needs.

2. Static Effort:

     Involves a prolonged state of contraction in a fixed position.
     Restricts blood flow to the muscle tissue.

Musculoskeletal System: Metabolism

 Metabolism is the chemical process of conversion of food into mechanical work
  and heat.

 Metabolism never stops. It supplies the necessary energy for breathing, blood
  circulation, digesting food, cell mechanics, regulating body temperature, and
  functionality of the musculoskeletal system.

UNIVERSITY OF FLORIDA HERBERT WERTHEIM COLLEGE OF ENGINEERING  15
Musculoskeletal System: Metabolism

 Aerobic work: If sufficient oxygen is supplied to the muscle, the pyruvic acid is
  broken down into water and carbon dioxide, releasing large amounts of ATP.

 Anaerobic work: Under insufficient oxygen supply, pyruvic acid is converted into
  lactic acid while ATP is regenerated. Latic acid accumulation between muscle
  fibers causes muscular fatigue and pain.

Musculoskeletal System: Metabolism

 Kilocalorie (kcal): measure of energy requirement for physical activity.
 The average adult needs 2,000 Ł 2,500 kcal/day.
 Total Metabolism = Basal Metabolism + Digestive Metabolism + Activity Metabolism
 Basal Metabolism: the minimum steady energy consumption to maintain involuntary

  activity (excluding digestion).

      For males (70 kg) about 1700 kcal/day
      For females (60 kg) about 1400 kcal/day

 Digestive metabolism: The amount of energy consumed by the digestive system. Can
  be estimated as 10% of the sum of basal and activity metabolisms.

 Activity Metabolism: The amount of energy is required to perform daily or work
  activities.
