Exercise 2

A manufacturing facility wants to know how many hours during an 8-hr day a machine is
being serviced by an operator. You conducted a pilot work sampling study over one day and
obtained the following data:

Operator puts in raw material  45

Operator adjusts the settings  25

Operator removes finished product 12

Machine runs automatically     145

Machine idle                   75

    Provide management with a 99% confidence interval on the machine servicing time.
    There is a total of 302 observations. Out of those, 45 + 25 + 12 = 82 observations are
related to machine servicing.

 p(servicing) = 82/302 = 0.272 and with z(/2) = 2.576
 l(servicing) = z(/2) × [p(1 - p)/n] = 0.066
 p(servicing) = 0.272 ± 0.066
 Servicing time = (0.272 ± 0.066) × 8 = 2.176 ± 0.528 hours
(We multiplied by 8 because the problem asks for the number of hours not just the percentage
of time the machine is being serviced)
