Occupational Biomechanics

 Biomechanics : uses the laws of physics and engineering mechanics to describe:

 The motions of various body segments ( kinematics ) and

 Understand the effects of forces and moments acting on the body (  kinetics ).

 Two major branches of biomechanics:

      Kinematics: the study of motion - used in correlation with measurement of body movements (motion
        capture ... ). It does not consider "force " component.

      Kinetics: the study of what causes motion - used in correlation with force / torque measurements
        (force plates, load cells ... ). It includes both force and movement.

               Based measurements which are external to the body.

 Occupational biomechanics : studies the physical interaction of workers with their tools,
  machines, and materials to enhance worker performance while minimizing risk of
  musculoskeletal injury.

 Standard Anatomical Posture:

      Standing erect, face forward, holding arms down at side with palms facing forward.

 Other terms used in kinesiology that describe movement:

      Flexion -decreasing the angle between two bones
      Extension -increasing the angle between two bones
      Abduction -moving away laterally from mid -body
      Adduction -moving toward mid -body
      Rotation -movement of a bone around its long axis
      Pronation: face -down, or palm -down position
      Supination: face -up, or palm -up position

Conventions for Moments and Forces

 Equilibrium: If a body is not in motion, the sum of all forces and moments acting
  on it must be equal to zero.

      Translational equilibrium:  Forces = 0
      Rotational equilibrium:  Moments = 0

 Resultant or External:

      What the world does to the body (e.g. gravity, contact loading).

 Reactive or Internal:

      What the body does in response (e.g. muscle activation, ligament stretch).

 In equilibrium: Resultant = Reactive
 Force is measured in Newtons (N), where 1 kg force = 9.81 N

Solving a Biomechanics Model

 There are two approaches to solving a biomechanical model:

    1. Segment -to-segment: treats each body segment as an element in a kinetic
         chain and works back to all joints affected when handling loads.

    2. One segment: treats all segments as one unit.
