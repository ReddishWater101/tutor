Introduction

 Work sampling: technique used to determine the amount of time devoted to the various activities
  that constitute a job

 Developed by LHC Tippett in 1934 in order to study the operations in the textile industry

 Work sampling: take a large number of observations at random intervals and use that to
  approximate the desired time

       What machines and equipment are being used

 Work sampling: measurements are made over a long period of time, from days to weeks and

Determine observation frequency

 When should the observations be taken?
 Try to spread out your observations

      Assume your desired n = 500 samples
      Assume your available time is 20 days
      Target (around) 25 observations per day

 What factors affect the observation frequency?

      Number of analysts available
      Location of the work
      Difficulty of access
      Method of measurement

 Want to avoid biasing: observations synchronous with the process

      Do not always go at the same times
      Do not go at fixed intervals for cyclical work

 How to take observations

      Want to take observations randomly at different times of day
      Need to include normal fluctuations in production

              Usually over two to four weeks, if possible

 Identify random times (e.g., using Excel)

      RAND()
      RAND()*number of hours
      Convert to time
      Eliminate times outside of work hours
