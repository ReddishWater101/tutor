From Biomechanics to Task Evaluation

 Compare job demands to human capacity.

      Compare external moments to joint strength (maximum internal moment).
      Use appropriate strength data (i.e. in same posture).

 Two types of evaluations:

      Compare external moments with an individual's joint strength (e.g. screening).
      Determine what percentage of the population is capable of performing the job.

 Ultimate question: Is the task or load acceptable?

Unacceptable conditions lead to:

 Work-Related Musculoskeletal Disorders (WMSD or MSD) are pain or injuries
  in muscles, joints, ligaments, tendons, and/or nerves.

      Most affected body parts are the back (46.5%) and shoulder (13.3%)

 Physical factors are the leading cause of WMSDs.

Preventing WMSDs

 Institute Controls
     Engineering Controls: changes made to the work environment, layout, tools,
      or equipment or changing the way a job is performed to avoid injuries.

     Administrative Controls: Work practices and policies by the administration to
      reduce risk of injury.

     Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Equipment worn by the worker to
      reduce risk of injury.
