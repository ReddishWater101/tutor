Performance rate the operator

 Even if you tried to find a qualified operator, they may not always be performing the
  standard method at the standard rate

      The operator may be going slower
      The operator may be going faster

 You need to give the operator a performance rating based on how close the operator is to
  the standard performance

 Different ways to rate performance

 At each cycle: not practical and hard to do

 For each element:

 Typically done for very long and multi -part jobs (cycle time > 30 min)

 For the whole operation:                                      Elemental times                     Normal time

        Typically used for shorter, more homogeneous jobs

 Rating can be open to criticism                                                Rating > 100

 Different analysts may give different ratings                                  Rating = 100
 Analyst may be biased                                                               Rating < 100

How to rate an operator

 Rating methods

     Speed rating
     Westinghouse system

 Speed Rating:

     Consider only the rate of work per unit time

           Easiest to apply
           Operator is rated based on the speed of work
           Analyst needs to have complete knowledge of the job and form a mental model of it

 The Westinghouse system

     One of the oldest rating systems
     Considers four factors

           Skill
           Effort
           Conditions
           Consistency

 Skill: proficiency at following a given method

      Based on operator experience, training, and own aptitude
      Increases over time
      Can vary from job to job and over time

Types of allowances

 Constant allowances

      Constant values added for any type of job
      Personal needs (5%) + basic fatigue (4%) = 9%

 Variable fatigue allowances

      Could be physical and/or psychological
      Main contributors:

             Abnormal posture
             Muscular force
             Atmospheric conditions
             Noise level
             Illumination level
             Visual strain
             Mental strain
             Monotony

 Special allowances (Unavoidable delays)

Final notes

 What if you just want the standard time for each element
  alone?

      Apply a rating and allowance to each one separately, as applicable

 What do you do if you have more than one worker or multiple
  stations?

      Get the standard time for each element
      Create a precedence chart before calculating the final standard time

 Hawthorne effect: workers change their behavior when they
  know they are being watched

      Named after the Hawthorne Works in Chicago where the studies were
        done

      Big problem with time studies
