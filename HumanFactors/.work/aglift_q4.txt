   1. What is the difference between Sense, Sensation, and Perception? (3 points)

   Sense: physical system that receives physical stimulation from surrounding environment and trans-
   lates that stimulation into an electrochemical message
   Sensation: detecting physical energy in the environment and encoding it as neural signals. Neurons
   transmit (and processes) the information from the sense organ to the brain.
   Perception: processing of information done by the brain ­ mental processes that organize and
   interpret sensory information that has been transmitted to the brain.
