Grasp

 Gain control of an object; often followed by Move.

     1. Pick up grasp: grasp an individual object

             A. By itself (most common)
             B. Small object lying against the surface (like a small paper)
             C. Cylindrical objects (like pens or markers next to each other)

     2. Regrasp: change grasp without relinquishing control; object stays in your hand
     3. Transfer: control transferred from one hand to the other
     4. Select: objects jumbled with each other such that search and select often occur

             A. Large
             B. Medium
             C. Small

     5. Contact: contact or sliding grasp (like pressing a button); your fingers to do not really hold
          on to anything

Position

 Align, orient, and engage one object with another object. Depends on:

       Class of fit (loose, close, exact):

               · Loose: like a cup to its saucer
               · Close: like a marker cap to a marker
               · Exact: like a key to its keyhole

       Symmetry (symmetrical, semi-symmetrical, non- symmetrical)

               · Symmetrical: infinity of ways to insert the object (like a pen inside a cup)
               · Semi-symmetrical: finite ways to insert the object (like a paper into a rectangular cardboard box)
               · Non-symmetrical: one and only one way to insert the object (like a key inside a keyhole)

       Ease of handling: how easy the object is to hold (slippery, dangerous, sharp...)

Eye travel time and focus

 Eye travel time = time to move the eyes = 15.2 (T/D) TMU

     Where T is the distance traveled by the eye and D is the perpendicular
      distance from the eye to the line of travel

 Maximum of 20 TMU
 Only use when explicitly stated
 Always place in right hand column
 Symbol: ET(T/D)

Remove non-limiting values

 Analyze the fundamental motions

    1. Align the LH and RH motions in terms of time

           Keep the order and alignment, regardless of the time

    2. If there are no simultaneous motions, move to step 6 (standard time)
    3. If there are simultaneous motions: check if they can be done at the same time

           If yes, circle the longer motion (limiting) and consider that time only
           If no, circle both motions and consider both times

Calculate standard time

 Sum all the individual (non-simultaneous) motions and all the circled
 simultaneous motions

 Final result is in TMU
 Can convert to

     Seconds: x0.036
     Minutes: x0.0006
     Hours: x0.00001

 Apply the appropriate allowance to obtain the standard time
