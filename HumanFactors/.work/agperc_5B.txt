2 . Phorias

 Some people eyes converge too much, while others tend not to converge enough        .

 Such conditions are called phorias .

 People with phorias also see one image but experience notable stress to the eye

muscles, making them more vulnerable to rapidly developing visual fatigue.

3 . Age

 With age, ciliary muscles weaken and visual performance declines.
 A young adult normally reads from a distance of about 30 cm ( 1 ft ). At 60 , the

  near point of accommodation is around 83 cm ( 2.75 ft).
 In industry, reading requirements, including character size, may be based on the

  limiting age of the expected user population.

9 . Depth Perception: Accommodation

 Ability to focus objects at different distances
  through involuntary contraction/relaxation of
  ciliary muscles (reshaping lens)

 To focus on far objects, lens flattens, and
  muscles relaxes (light rays are nearly parallel;
  limited refraction for focus)

 To focus on near objects, lens become
  rounded and muscle contract (light rays
  diverge; greater refraction is needed for
  focus)

      Can be fatiguing !

Glare

 Proper workplace lighting reduces visual fatigue and errors.
 Glare can reduce contrast sensitivity, increase eye strain, and lower

   productivity.
 Glare can slow reaction times, obscure critical information (e.g., on

   screens or control panels).
 Older adults and individuals with visual impairments are more

   sensitive to glare. Addressing glare supports universal design
   principles and inclusivity in the workplace.
